How to Compress PDF Online

By ZetRocks Team • Jan 15, 2025 • 6–8 min read

Learn the best techniques to reduce PDF file sizes without compromising quality. Whether you’re emailing documents, uploading assignments, or archiving reports, these tips keep your PDFs crisp and lightweight.

Link copied!

Why compress a PDF?

Large PDFs upload slowly and get blocked by size limits. Compression optimizes images, fonts, and metadata for a much smaller file that still looks clear.

Quick method (fastest)

  1. Open the ZetRocks PDF Compressor.
  2. Drop your PDF or choose it from your device.
  3. Select quality: Balanced (default), or High for scans, Light for fine text/diagrams.
  4. Click Compress and download.

Why PDFs get big

  • Heavy images: Large, uncompressed photos dominate size.
  • Scans: Each page is an image; DPI and color increase weight.
  • Fonts & extras: Many fonts, metadata, and thumbnails add up.

Best settings

  • Downscale images: 150–200 DPI for screen; 300 DPI for print.
  • Right format: JPEG for photos, PNG for sharp graphics.
  • Grayscale scans: Use when color isn’t needed.

Quick checklist

  • Start with Balanced, verify on mobile.
  • Remove unused pages/metadata.
  • Subset fonts; avoid extra weights.

FAQ

Does text get blurry? No—images are affected most; vector text stays sharp.

Is it private? Yes—our compressor works in‑browser; files stay on your device.

Target size? Resumes/forms: 100–500 KB. Image‑heavy reports: under ~5 MB.
