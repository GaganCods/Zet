Image Optimization Guide: Master Speed Without Losing Visual Appeal

By ZetRocks Team • Aug 21, 2025 • 7 min read

Faster pages, happier users, and better SEO. Learn how to pick the right format, compress smartly, resize responsibly, and deliver images efficiently.

Why image optimization matters

Images often account for the largest share of page weight. Optimizing them improves perceived performance, Core Web Vitals (LCP/CLS), and conversion. The goal is to reduce bytes without harming perceived quality.

Choose the right format

  • JPG: Photographs and complex gradients. Adjustable quality, small file sizes.
  • PNG: Graphics with sharp edges, transparency, or text.
  • WebP: Modern lossy/lossless, better compression than JPG/PNG in most cases.
  • AVIF: Next‑gen with excellent compression; still maturing in tooling and support.

Compress smartly

Start from a high‑quality source. Lower quality gradually until differences are barely noticeable. Avoid double compression. For PNGs, use lossless compression with palette reduction when possible.

Resize to display size

Do not ship 4000px images for a 1200px layout. Export multiple sizes and use responsive techniques.

<img srcset="image-800.jpg 800w, image-1200.jpg 1200w, image-1600.jpg 1600w"
     sizes="(max-width: 768px) 100vw, 1200px"
     src="image-1200.jpg" alt="...">

Delivery best practices

  • Lazy‑load offscreen images: loading="lazy".
  • Set explicit width/height to avoid layout shifts.
  • Use <picture> for modern formats with fallbacks.
  • Cache and use a CDN when possible.
